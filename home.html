About Space Medicine
Aerospace medicine is a specialist field of medicine that studies medical issues that arise during human flight both inside and outside of the atmosphere (aviation medicine).
WHAT
Most people agree that Paul Bert, a French physiologist from the 19th century, is the founder of contemporary aviation medicine. His groundbreaking discoveries of how balloonists were affected by high and low air pressure were widely employed starting in World War II and led to a large and active research program.
WHO
Promoting human efficacy and safety in the face of aerospace flight's extremes—high temperatures, low air pressure, radiation, noise, vibrations, oxygen deprivation, and powerful acceleration and deceleration forces—is the ultimate goal of this specialty.
WHY
Know more about Space Medicine

Telemedicine

Telemedicine enables astronauts to consult with healthcare professionals on Earth in real-time, facilitating diagnosis and treatment of medical issues during space missions.

Remote Surgery

Remote surgery allows surgeons on Earth to perform surgical procedures on astronauts in space using robotic systems, ensuring timely medical interventions.

Space Pharmacology

Space pharmacology involves the study of how medications interact with the human body in space, helping to develop drugs tailored for use in microgravity environments.
